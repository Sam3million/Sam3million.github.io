This was my team's Senior Design project at UCF. Monoball is a single rotor, spherically enclosed,
ducted camera drone meant for reconaissance and search-and-rescue operations. The team was composed
of 4 Aerospace Engineering majors (including myself), and 2 Computer Engineering majors.

# Background

The Monoball project began in Fall 2025 as a UCF Senior Design project sponsored by Lockheed Martin.
I applied for it as my top pick and was selected to be a part of the team.

The project was pitched as a reconaissance and search-and-rescue drone. The main use case is
entering environments where it would be dangerous for humans to go. For example, a collapsed building
or wildfire. The protective outer shell will allow the drone to bump into walls and obstacles
in these environments. It will transmit a live video feed back to the operator so they can
navigate and explore. The sponsor also requested that it have a rolling mode, where
the drone could roll on the ground instead of flying. This theoretically saves battery life
and allows for traversal in height-constrained environments.

# Requirements

The team met with the sponsor to generate a list of requirements. Some of those included:
- 30 minute flight time
- Beach ball sized or smaller
- Roll mode
- High quality camera feed
- Ducted propeller
- GPS

# Design

We each conducted technology studies into different areas to gain an understanding of what
the project would entail. I looked into drone computing hardware, including things
like flight controllers, sensors, and RF transmitters. My teammates looked into
topics such as propulsion, theory, software, and existing solutions.

From this, we came up with initial design concepts. The initial idea
was essentially a block of PLA where the internals would be mounted,
surrounded by a geodesic outer shell. The drone is lifted by a
single upwards facing propeller and moves with 4 control surfaces
mounted near the bottom.

![Initial Design](/assets/monoball/initial-design.png)
*Initial Design*

The main issues with this design were weight and mounting space.
The thick PLA construction of the internal components and the outer shell 
would have been too heavy for a single motor to lift. There also wasn't enough mounting space
for all the internal components 

## Revision 2

Inspired by a research paper that built a similar monocopter drone, the team did a redesign
prioritizing weight. The new design uses modular plates that can be mounted on a series
of carbon fiber tubes. Small flanges and walls were later added to the top plate to provide more
mounting area.
